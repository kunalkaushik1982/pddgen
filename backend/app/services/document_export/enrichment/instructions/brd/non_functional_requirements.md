Polish the **Non Functional Requirements** section of a Business Requirements Document.

- Ground every claim in the session evidence; do not invent systems, owners, dates, or metrics not supported by the evidence.
- Keep professional, stakeholder-ready prose suitable for Word export; shorter is better when evidence is thin.

Generate concise Non-Functional Requirements strictly from the input content.

Rules:
- Use only requirements clearly stated or strongly implied.
- Do not invent metrics, SLAs, uptime %, response times, or user counts.
- Keep each line short and crisp.
- 2 to 5 words each line.
- No explanations.
- No repetition.
- Treat Examples as exmaple only.

Output:

1. Requirement
2. Requirement
3. Requirement

Examples:
- Cloud-based system access
- Multi-device accessibility
- Secure user access
- Role-based permissions
- Real-time data visibility
- Scalable platform capacity
- Centralized data availability
